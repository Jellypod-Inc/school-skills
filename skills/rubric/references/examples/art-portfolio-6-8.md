# Rubric: Middle School Art Portfolio (5-piece selection)

**Assignment:** Curate a portfolio of 5 pieces from the quarter that represent your growth. Include an artist statement (1 page). Demonstrate range of media, concept development, and reflection on your process.

**Alignment:** SOLO Taxonomy · 5 criteria x 3 levels · qualitative (no points)

| Criterion | Approaching | Meeting | Exceeding |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Concept** *SOLO 2: Unistructural* | Pieces share no visible idea or theme; artist statement does not name a concept. | Pieces relate to a stated concept; artist statement names the theme and gives one example per piece. | Concept evolves across pieces; artist statement traces how early work shaped later decisions, citing specific pieces. |
| **Craft & Technique** *SOLO 3: Multistructural* | Technique inconsistent; fundamentals (line, value, proportion) are unclear across multiple pieces. | Fundamentals demonstrated in at least 3 of 5 pieces; some evidence of intentional technique choices. | Technique serves the concept — student chooses tools and methods to match meaning; fundamentals strong across all 5 pieces. |
| **Use of Medium** *SOLO 2: Unistructural* | One medium used across all pieces with no variation; or multiple media used superficially. | At least 2 media represented; each used in ways that fit its properties (e.g., watercolor for transparency, graphite for precision). | 3+ media represented; student pushes a medium beyond default uses; demonstrates understanding of each medium's affordances. |
| **Composition** *SOLO 3: Multistructural* | Pieces center subjects without attention to negative space, balance, or focal point. | Most pieces show intentional composition — focal point identifiable, balance considered. | Composition choices reinforce concept across the portfolio; student can explain why each choice was made (framing, rule of thirds, cropping, etc.). |
| **Reflection** *SOLO 4: Relational* | Artist statement describes what pieces are without evaluation. | Artist statement names one success and one challenge per piece or for the portfolio as a whole. | Artist statement connects pieces to each other and to the student's larger artistic goals; identifies specific skills gained and what to try next. |

**Notes for teachers:** SOLO alignment works well for art because the question is "how integrated is the student's thinking across pieces?" rather than cognitive demand. No points deliberately — middle-school art is more developmentally productive when framed as growth. If your school requires a letter grade, map Approaching=C, Meeting=B, Exceeding=A; require Meeting on ≥4 of 5 criteria for B, etc.
