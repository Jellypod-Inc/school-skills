# Rubric: Middle-School Math Problem Set (6-8 problems, multi-step)

**Assignment:** Solve the 6 problems on the handout. Show all work. For at least 3 problems, write 2-3 sentences explaining your strategy and justifying your answer.

**Alignment:** Webb's DOK + CCSS Math Practices · 4 criteria x 4 levels · 100 points · weighted 25/25/30/20

| Criterion | 1 Beginning | 2 Developing | 3 Proficient | 4 Exemplary |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Strategy (25 pts)** *DOK 2-3 · MP1* | No strategy visible; attempts only first step of most problems. (6 pts) | Strategy appears on 2-3 problems; strategy is not matched to the problem type. (13 pts) | Strategy appears on most problems and fits the problem type (e.g., ratio for proportional reasoning, equation for unknown). (19 pts) | Strategy chosen deliberately; uses representations (tables, diagrams, equations) to match the problem; shows evidence of checking whether the strategy makes sense. (25 pts) |
| **Execution (25 pts)** *DOK 1-2 · MP6* | Calculations wrong on most problems; units missing. (6 pts) | 2-3 problems correct; arithmetic errors on others; units inconsistent. (13 pts) | Most problems correct; minor arithmetic errors only; units labeled. (19 pts) | All problems correct; work is organized and traceable; units labeled throughout; answers checked for reasonableness. (25 pts) |
| **Justification (30 pts)** *DOK 3 · MP3* | No written justification or justification repeats the answer. (8 pts) | Justification present on 1-2 problems; cites a rule without explaining why it applies. (16 pts) | Justification present on required 3 problems; explains WHY the strategy works using mathematical reasoning (not just "I did this because it worked"). (23 pts) | Justification on all problems where warranted; uses precise mathematical language; connects to underlying concepts (e.g., "because the ratio is constant," "because addition is commutative"). (30 pts) |
| **Communication (20 pts)** *MP6* | Work disorganized; answers not distinguishable from scratch work. (5 pts) | Work mostly organized; answers boxed or circled on some problems. (10 pts) | Each problem's work is organized linearly; final answers clearly marked; notation consistent. (15 pts) | Work flows logically; notation is precise (equal signs used correctly, parentheses where needed); a classmate could follow the reasoning without explanation. (20 pts) |

**Column totals (per level):** 25 / 52 / 76 / 100.

**Notes for teachers:** DOK tags reflect the cognitive demand of each criterion: Strategy and Justification are Strategic Thinking (DOK 3); Execution and Communication are skills/concepts (DOK 1-2). CCSS MP3 (construct arguments) is explicitly called out because the 3-problem justification requirement is the main vehicle for that practice in a problem set. For classes on standards-based grading, collapse the 4 levels to Approaching / Meeting / Exceeding and drop the point column.
