# Rubric: 7th-Grade Group Presentation (Historical Figure)

**Assignment:** In teams of 3-4, research a historical figure of your choice (teacher-approved) and present a 6-8 minute multimedia presentation to the class. Each team member must speak and contribute a specific research area.

**Alignment:** Bloom's + CCSS.ELA-Literacy.SL.7 · 5 criteria x 4 levels · 100 points · weighted 25/15/15/15/30 · weighting reflects primary emphasis on content + individual accountability

| Criterion | 1 Beginning | 2 Developing | 3 Proficient | 4 Exemplary |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Content Quality (25 pts)** *Analyze — Bloom's 4 · CCSS.ELA.W.7.7* | Facts listed without context; 0-1 credible sources. (6 pts) | Some facts tied to the figure's life; 2 sources, not all credible. (13 pts) | Facts organized chronologically or thematically; 3+ credible sources (not just Wikipedia); explains significance. (19 pts) | Integrates primary and secondary sources; situates the figure within their historical context; explains why the figure matters now. (25 pts) |
| **Organization (15 pts)** *CCSS.ELA.SL.7.4* | No clear structure; presentation jumps between topics. (4 pts) | Opening present; middle disorganized; ending abrupt. (8 pts) | Clear opening, middle, closing; transitions between speakers named. (11 pts) | Opening hooks audience; transitions are smooth and signal progression; closing synthesizes and invites reflection. (15 pts) |
| **Delivery (15 pts)** *CCSS.ELA.SL.7.4* | Reading from notes; minimal eye contact; inaudible. (4 pts) | Reads from slides; some eye contact; volume inconsistent. (8 pts) | Speaks from notes rather than reading verbatim; audible to whole class; appropriate pace. (11 pts) | Engages audience; varied pace and tone; all team members make eye contact; handles disfluencies professionally. (15 pts) |
| **Visuals (15 pts)** *Apply — Bloom's 3* | Slides text-heavy (>50 words per slide); no images, or images are decorative only. (4 pts) | Mix of text and images; some slides still text-heavy; citations missing. (8 pts) | Slides follow 6x6 rule (≤6 bullets, ≤6 words per bullet); images relevant and captioned; sources cited on each slide using them. (11 pts) | Slides amplify rather than repeat spoken content; visual hierarchy clear; image selection is deliberate and thought-provoking; full citation slide at the end. (15 pts) |
| **Individual Contribution (30 pts)** *Evaluate — Bloom's 5* | Cannot identify what this student researched or produced; spoke <1 minute OR did not speak. (8 pts) | Spoke 1-2 minutes; contribution to preparation partially documented. (16 pts) | Spoke 2-3 minutes on a clearly defined research area; team contribution log shows equal participation in prep. (23 pts) | Spoke 2-3 minutes with clear ownership of a research area; can answer Q&A on their topic; team log AND peer evaluation confirm substantive, balanced contribution. (30 pts) |

**Companion peer-evaluation form (per team member, submitted individually):**

Rate each teammate (including yourself) on a 1-5 scale for each:

1. Contributed their fair share of research and preparation.
2. Showed up to team meetings prepared.
3. Communicated respectfully and responded to messages.
4. Delivered their assigned speaking role.
5. I would want to work with this person on another project.

Write 1-2 sentences per teammate explaining the rating. The teacher uses peer-eval data to calibrate the Individual Contribution criterion — substantial discrepancies between self and peer ratings trigger a follow-up conversation.

**Notes for teachers:** The 30-point weight on Individual Contribution is deliberate — research on group projects consistently finds that without individual accountability, free-riding tanks learning for stronger students. The peer-eval form is not used to assign grades directly but to surface misalignment.
