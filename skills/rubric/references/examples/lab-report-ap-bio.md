# Rubric: AP Biology Lab Report (Enzyme Kinetics)

**Assignment:** Design and conduct an investigation into the effect of temperature OR pH on enzyme (catalase) activity. Produce a formal lab report including background, methods, data/analysis, and conclusion. Follow AP Biology lab report conventions.

**Alignment:** NGSS Science & Engineering Practices · 5 criteria x 4 levels · 100 points · weighted 15/20/30/20/15

| Criterion | 1 Beginning | 2 Developing | 3 Proficient | 4 Exemplary |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Hypothesis & Background (15 pts)** *NGSS SEP 1* | No testable hypothesis; background missing or off-topic. (4 pts) | Hypothesis vague or not testable; background cites 0-1 sources. (8 pts) | Testable hypothesis with predicted direction; background cites 2+ sources and defines key terms (substrate, catalyst, denaturation). (11 pts) | Hypothesis explicitly links to mechanism; background synthesizes 3+ primary sources and situates the question within enzyme kinetics theory. (15 pts) |
| **Methods (20 pts)** *NGSS SEP 3* | Procedure incomplete; variables unidentified; not reproducible. (5 pts) | Procedure listed but missing controls or replicates; variables partially identified. (10 pts) | Clear procedure with IV, DV, controls, and ≥3 trials; reproducible by another student. (15 pts) | Procedure justifies each design choice, identifies potential confounds, and cites equipment precision. (20 pts) |
| **Data & Analysis (30 pts)** *NGSS SEP 4* | Data presented without organization; no calculations; no graph. (8 pts) | Data table present; partial analysis; graph has errors (axes unlabeled, wrong type). (16 pts) | Organized data table; correct graph type (line/bar) with labeled axes and units; mean + standard deviation reported; appropriate statistical test selected. (23 pts) | Quantitative analysis includes uncertainty; graph choices justified; clear pattern described with reference to kinetics (Vmax, Km concepts where applicable). (30 pts) |
| **Conclusion (20 pts)** *NGSS SEP 6 + 7* | Restates hypothesis without reference to data. (5 pts) | Conclusion references data but does not connect to hypothesis or mechanism. (10 pts) | Accepts/rejects hypothesis with specific data references; proposes at least one source of error. (15 pts) | Integrates findings with biological mechanism; evaluates sources of error quantitatively; proposes next investigation grounded in results. (20 pts) |
| **Scientific Communication (15 pts)** *NGSS SEP 8* | Informal tone; no figure captions; citations missing. (4 pts) | Mostly third-person past tense; inconsistent figure captions; citations present but inconsistent. (8 pts) | Third-person past tense throughout; all figures captioned and referenced in text; CSE or APA citations consistent. (11 pts) | Publication-quality prose; figures and tables integrated seamlessly; concise and precise technical language. (15 pts) |

**Column totals (per level across all criteria):** 26 / 52 / 75 / 100.

**Notes for teachers:** NGSS SEPs are the primary alignment here because this is a lab practice, not a content standard. For AP classes, you may additionally tag with AP Biology Science Practices 1-6. The 30-pt weight on Data & Analysis reflects AP's emphasis on quantitative reasoning.
