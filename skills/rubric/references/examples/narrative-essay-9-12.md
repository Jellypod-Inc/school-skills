# Rubric: 9-12 Narrative Essay

**Assignment:** Write a 3-5 page personal narrative about a formative experience. Use vivid sensory detail, a clear arc, and at least two distinct scenes. Target audience: general reader outside your immediate life.

**Alignment:** Bloom's Taxonomy · 4 criteria x 4 levels · 100 points · equal weights (25 pts/criterion)

| Criterion | 1 Beginning (6 pts) | 2 Developing (13 pts) | 3 Proficient (19 pts) | 4 Exemplary (25 pts) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Narrative Arc & Thesis** *Create — Bloom's 6* | Events listed chronologically with no clear significance; no discernible arc. | Arc present but uneven; rising action or resolution underdeveloped. | Clear arc (exposition -> conflict -> resolution); stated or implied theme. | Deliberate arc with purposeful pacing; theme emerges organically from events, not stated. |
| **Character & Setting** *Analyze — Bloom's 4* | Characters are names only; setting unspecified. | Characters described flatly; setting named but not shown. | Characters have at least one distinguishing action or line; setting uses 2+ sensory details. | Characters revealed through action and dialogue; setting integrated with mood across multiple scenes. |
| **Language & Voice** *Evaluate — Bloom's 5* | Generic diction; no consistent voice. | Occasional specific word choice; voice inconsistent. | Voice is consistent and appropriate; uses specific, concrete nouns and active verbs. | Distinctive voice sustained throughout; deliberate rhetorical choices (rhythm, imagery, figurative language) amplify meaning. |
| **Conventions** *Apply — Bloom's 3* | Frequent errors impede meaning; no paragraphing. | Errors occasional but intact meaning; paragraphing uneven. | Largely error-free; paragraphing supports arc; MLA formatted. | Precise, varied syntax; deliberate punctuation for effect; flawless MLA. |

**Notes for teachers:** This rubric assumes students have had prior instruction on narrative arc (Freytag), sensory detail, and MLA. For ELL students, the "Conventions" criterion can be modified to assess only errors that impede meaning. Level 1 rarely applies to submitted drafts in a 9-12 context — most submissions land at Developing or above.
