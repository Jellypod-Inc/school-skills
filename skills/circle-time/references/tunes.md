# Public-Domain Tune Catalog

Use these tunes as the melody for ORIGINAL lyrics. Never include the traditional lyrics of any song with living authorship or known copyright. Every tune below is confirmed public domain (pre-1900 composition or traditional folk melody).

## How to use

1. Pick a tune whose meter matches the theme vocabulary you want.
2. Compose fresh lyrics that fit the meter + syllable count per line.
3. Attribute the melody in the plan as: _"to the tune of <Tune Name>"_.
4. **Never output the original traditional lyrics alongside your composition.** Do not print "Twinkle, twinkle, little star, how I wonder what you are" — write new words instead. Even though the traditional lyrics are themselves public domain in most cases, skipping them avoids confusion about whose words are whose.
5. Include simple hand motions when they fit (most PreK-K tunes benefit from motions).

## Catalog

### Twinkle, Twinkle, Little Star
- **Melody status:** Public domain (French folk tune "Ah! vous dirai-je, maman," 1761).
- **Meter:** 7-7-7-7-7-7 syllables across 6 short lines (AABB CC).
- **Age fit:** PreK-2.
- **Theme fit:** Anything concrete and observable — stars, weather, numbers, letters, animals, apples, feelings.
- **Composition hint:** Line 1 names the concept twice; line 2 describes it; lines 3-4 add detail; lines 5-6 repeat or close.

### Mary Had a Little Lamb
- **Melody status:** Public domain (Lowell Mason, 1830).
- **Meter:** 7-6-7-6 (four-line verse).
- **Age fit:** PreK-1.
- **Theme fit:** A character + an action (helpers, animals, weekday routines, feelings).
- **Composition hint:** Line 1 introduces a character; line 2 their trait; lines 3-4 close the thought.

### The Wheels on the Bus (use "Mulberry Bush" melody, confirmed public domain)
- **Melody status:** The melody "Here We Go Round the Mulberry Bush" is confirmed public domain and is the same tune modern ears hear as "Wheels on the Bus." Attribute as "to the tune of The Wheels on the Bus / Mulberry Bush."
- **Meter:** 8-7-8-7 with repeating refrain.
- **Age fit:** PreK-K.
- **Theme fit:** Repetitive action verbs ("The [thing] on the [place] goes [verb] [verb] [verb]"). Excellent for body-part songs, community-helpers, weather.

### Frère Jacques (Are You Sleeping?)
- **Melody status:** Public domain (French nursery, 18th century).
- **Meter:** 4-4-4-4-6-6-4-4 (round-friendly).
- **Age fit:** K-2.
- **Theme fit:** Call-and-response, bilingual options (French; Spanish version "Martinillo" also public domain). Great for days-of-the-week and counting.

## Composition rules

- **Keep lines concrete.** PreK-K verses should name a thing children can see or touch.
- **Repeat the theme word** at least twice across the verses so children anchor it.
- **One vocabulary stretch per song for K-2** is the max; more and you lose the melody hook.
- **Check syllable count out loud.** If it doesn't sing, it won't work.
- **Write 2+ verses** so there's musical closure; 4-line minimum for the skill output.

## Forbidden (copyrighted — DO NOT USE)

Never write lyrics to or reference these by name:

- Anything by The Wiggles, Raffi, Laurie Berkner, Kidz Bop, Disney, Sesame Workshop, The Learning Station, Super Simple Songs (post-1997 originals), Baby Shark (Pinkfong), Pete the Cat songs, Daniel Tiger theme, Hap Palmer, Ella Jenkins compositions where copyright is still held.
- "Let It Go," "You've Got a Friend in Me," "Happy" (Pharrell), or any modern pop song.
- Any song you can't confirm is pre-1900 or traditional folk.

If unsure whether a tune is public domain, pick one from the catalog above.
