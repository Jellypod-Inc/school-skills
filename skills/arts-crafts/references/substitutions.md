# Supply Substitution Guide

Common swaps when a user's supplies are limited, missing, or age-gated. Always include inline substitutions in the Supply List ("white glue *or* glue stick").

## Adhesives

| If you have... | Also works | Age caveats |
|---|---|---|
| Glue stick | White school glue, double-sided tape, washi tape | Stick is safest 2+. Bottle glue 4-5+. |
| White school glue | Glue stick, tacky glue, Mod Podge | Tacky glue 5+; Mod Podge 5+ with smock. |
| Tacky glue | White glue (longer dry time), hot glue (adult only) | Tacky 5+. |
| Hot glue (adult only) | Tacky glue + overnight dry, double-sided foam tape | Never hand to a child. |
| Tape (masking / washi / double-sided) | Stapler (adult), brad fasteners | Staples = adult. Brads 5+. |

**No-glue substitutions:** double-sided tape, washi tape, sticker shapes, velcro dots, brad fasteners, staples (adult).

| Popsicle sticks | Coffee stirrers, rolled-paper tubes, chopsticks (ages 5+), tongue depressors | |

## Recycled and free-at-home

Default substitutes when user says "junk drawer", "no budget", "recycled only":

- Empty toilet-paper / paper-towel tubes
- Egg cartons (paper or foam)
- Cereal and tissue boxes (flat cardboard)
- Newspaper
- Junk-mail envelopes
- Magazine pages (for collage)
- Bottle caps
- Jar lids
- Scrap fabric / old t-shirts
- Brown paper bags
- Used wrapping paper / tissue
- Leaves, acorns, twigs, pinecones, smooth rocks (age 3+ with supervision)
- Dried pasta (large shapes only for under 5)
- Dry beans / rice (for shakers; adult-supervised under 4)

## No-scissors substitutes

When user says "no scissors":

- **Tearing** — all ages; strongest fine-motor benefit for 2-4.
- **Hole punch** — adult-operated for under 5; ages 5+ independent.
- **Pre-cut shapes** — teacher/parent cuts ahead; kids just assemble. Essential for classroom scale.
- **Washi-tape strips** — no cutting needed; tear by hand.
- **Sticker shapes** — foam stickers, dot stickers, shape stickers.
- **Fold-and-rip** — fold and tear along the crease for a "torn edge" effect.
- **Kids' serrated plastic knife** — for playdough and soft materials (ages 4+).

## Edible-craft allergen swaps

| Avoid | Substitute |
|---|---|
| Peanut butter | Sunflower-seed butter (SunButter), soy butter, tahini |
| Wheat pasta / flour | Gluten-free pasta, rice flour, chickpea flour |
| Dairy (yogurt, milk, butter) | Coconut yogurt, oat milk, coconut oil |
| Egg | Aquafaba (chickpea liquid), flax egg (1 tbsp flax + 3 tbsp water) |
| Marshmallow (gelatin) | Vegan marshmallow (Dandies, agar-based) |
| Soy | Coconut or sunflower alternatives |

Always flag allergens in the Safety Notes section of any edible craft, even with substitutions.
