# Fine-Motor Age Mapping

Use this to sanity-check that every step in a craft is physically achievable by the target age. When in doubt, anchor one age younger.

## Developmental snapshot per age

### Age 2
- Grasp: palmar / gross fist grasp, developing pincer.
- Cutting: none — cannot safely hold scissors.
- Folding: crumples, random folds, no intentional creases.
- Drawing: fist-grip crayon, scribbles, makes dots.
- Bilateral: one hand stabilizes while the other acts, but loosely.

### Age 3
- Grasp: pincer grasp established, tripod emerging.
- Cutting: snips edge of paper with blunt safety scissors (no line-following).
- Folding: folds paper in half with edges mismatched.
- Drawing: imitates vertical and horizontal lines, copies a circle.
- Bilateral: can hold paper steady while scribbling.

### Age 4
- Grasp: tripod grasp becoming stable.
- Cutting: cuts a short straight line with kid scissors (wobbly).
- Folding: folds paper in half reasonably; tries fold-to-match.
- Drawing: draws a recognizable person ("tadpole figure"), copies a cross.
- Bilateral: threads large beads on a pipe cleaner (not yet on string).

### Age 5
- Grasp: mature tripod.
- Cutting: cuts on a straight line; starts curves.
- Folding: folds paper in half matching edges; fold-and-crease.
- Drawing: draws a person with head + body + limbs; copies a square.
- Bilateral: glues with a glue stick independently; uses bottle glue with supervision.

### Age 6
- Grasp: mature tripod, speed improving.
- Cutting: cuts on a curve, cuts out large shapes (wobbly on corners).
- Folding: multi-step fold-in-half-twice; simple origami (kite base, dog head).
- Drawing: draws a recognizable person with features; writes name.
- Bilateral: threads a bead on string; sews in-and-out on pre-punched burlap with a plastic needle.

### Age 7
- Grasp: efficient, can sustain for 15-20 min.
- Cutting: cuts out detailed shapes, cuts on a curve accurately.
- Folding: 3-5-step origami (cup, boat, frog).
- Drawing: draws in proportion; writes legibly.
- Bilateral: measures with a ruler (to nearest half-inch), uses a hole punch.

### Age 8
- Grasp: adult-like.
- Cutting: precision cuts, fringe, multi-layer paper.
- Folding: multi-step origami (crane begins, boat complete).
- Drawing: adds detail, shading, perspective attempts.
- Bilateral: basic sewing with a real needle (1:1 supervision), simple weaving.

### Age 9
- Grasp: adult-like, fine control.
- Cutting: precision including interior cuts (poke-and-cut).
- Folding: consistent crane, complex origami (lily, balloon).
- Drawing: uses a compass, begins technical drawing.
- Bilateral: simple bookbinding, tying knots reliably, basic knitting with large needles.

### Age 10
- Grasp: full adult dexterity.
- Cutting: any paper-cutting task; fabric with fabric scissors.
- Folding: advanced origami, paper engineering, pop-up cards.
- Drawing: uses protractor, compass, technical templates.
- Bilateral: hand-sewing to complete a small plushie, machine sewing with supervision.

## Red flags — steps to avoid by age

| Age | Don't prescribe |
|---|---|
| 2 | Scissors of any kind, small parts, liquid-glue bottles, bead threading on string, any step requiring a straight line. |
| 3 | Cutting on a line, folding to match edges, small parts (<1.25"), bottle glue independently, bead threading on string. |
| 4 | Cutting out shapes, real sewing needles, multi-step origami, precise measurement. |
| 5 | Multi-step origami, real sewing needles, hot glue. |
| 6 | Real sewing needles without 1:1, knife-type tools. |
| 7 | Machine tools, solvent adhesives. |
| 8+ | Rotary cutters, solvent adhesives, power tools. |

If the user's desired project requires a skill beyond the target age, propose a simplified version or anchor one age younger.
